Heilsgeschichte: God's Redemptive History

Heilsgeschichte (German for "salvation history") emphasizes that God is not only the God of Israel but also the God of all nations, continuing His redemptive plan through key moments in Israel's history—even in times of failure and exile. This thread is visible through the reigns of major kings and the work of the prophets.

  • Genesis 12:1–3 – God's promise to Abraham to bless all nations through his seed.
  • Exodus 19:5–6 – Israel is called to be a kingdom of priests for the world.
  • 2 Samuel 7 – God's covenant with David, promising an everlasting kingdom.
  • 1 Kings 8 – Solomon's temple dedication includes prayers for foreigners, showing God's global concern.
  • Isaiah 2:2–4 – The mountain of the Lord will be exalted and all nations shall flow unto it.
  • Isaiah 49:6 – The Servant will be a light to the Gentiles and bring salvation to the ends of the earth.
  • Jonah – God's mercy on Nineveh, a non-Israelite city, showing His universal concern.
  • Micah 4:1–5 – All nations shall come to the Lord’s mountain seeking His ways.
  • Jeremiah 1:5–10 – Jeremiah is appointed a prophet to the nations, not just Israel.
  • Daniel 2 & 7 – God's kingdom will surpass all human empires and endure forever.

These passages reflect a consistent biblical vision: even when Israel fails, God’s covenantal plan moves forward. The kings, prophets, and even Israel’s suffering become instruments in a larger redemptive history for all people.
